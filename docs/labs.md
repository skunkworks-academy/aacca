# Labs and Tutorials

<div className="confidential-banner"><strong>Lab safety boundary:</strong> use synthetic or explicitly approved data. Never copy tenant names, identity numbers, payment details, lease documents, bank details or access credentials into public repositories or unapproved services.</div>

## Lab catalogue

### Lab 1 — Property process discovery
Map actors, trigger, steps, decisions, hand-offs, controls, exceptions and outcomes for maintenance, viewing requests or rental reminders. Produce a SIPOC and process flow.

### Lab 2 — System and data inventory
Record systems, owners, purpose, data domains, integration method and criticality. Mark the provisional source of truth and duplicates.

### Lab 3 — Data dictionary and quality rules
Define properties, units, tenants, leases, payments, arrears, vacancies, maintenance and documents. Test duplicate, missing and invalid synthetic records.

### Lab 4 — Azure cloud decision brief
Compare compute, storage, identity, networking, monitoring and governance. Record assumptions, cost drivers, responsibilities and risk.

### Lab 5 — Power Automate approval flow
Build a low-risk flow with trigger, conditions, approvals, notifications, owners, audit, error handling and rollback. Test approved, rejected and failure paths.

### Lab 6 — Canvas app intake prototype
Build a maintenance or viewing-request app using an approved test source. Configure gallery, detail and input screens, validation and role-appropriate access.

### Lab 7 — Power Query and reconciliation
Import synthetic records from two sources, standardise formats, remove duplicates, flag unmatched records and document a repeatable refresh.

### Lab 8 — Process-mining event log
Create a synthetic event log with case ID, activity and timestamp. Analyse duration, variants, rework and bottlenecks.

### Lab 9 — Executive dashboard
Build occupancy, collections, arrears, vacancy, maintenance and response-time measures. Add data-quality indicators and management interpretation.

### Lab 10 — Privacy and threat review
Classify personal, financial, legal and operational data. Define least privilege, retention, consent, audit and incident actions.

### Lab 11 — Governed AI proof of concept
Use approved or synthetic data only. Define purpose, human approval, prohibited use and monitoring. Test expected, ambiguous, unsafe and failure scenarios.

### Lab 12 — Capstone pilot rehearsal
Demonstrate the data baseline, dashboard and workflow. Present risks, costs, benefits, assumptions and the next-stage decision.

## Standard evidence for every lab

- objective and authorised scope;
- architecture or process diagram;
- synthetic input data;
- reproducible steps;
- expected and actual results;
- exception and failure tests;
- security and privacy controls;
- limitations and lessons learned;
- mentor or process-owner review.
