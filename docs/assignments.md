# Assignments and Evidence

| Assignment | Due | Deliverable | Acceptance criteria |
|---|---|---|---|
| A1 — IDR validation | Week 1 | Signed validation note and weekly schedule | Role, availability, credentials and priority processes confirmed. |
| A2 — Process baseline | Week 2 | Five process candidates and one detailed current-state map | Trigger, actors, decisions, controls, exceptions and measurable outcome shown. |
| A3 — System/data inventory | Week 3 | System register, data-source map and owners | Critical systems, data domains, source of truth and sensitivities recorded. |
| A4 — Cloud decision brief | Week 4 | Azure decision record and practice result | Assumptions, services, responsibilities, cost and governance explained. |
| A5 — Workflow design | Week 5 | Low-risk workflow map and automation design | Approval, exceptions, audit, failure and rollback included. |
| A6 — App and dashboard design | Week 6 | User stories, wireframes, data model and test plan | User need, permissions, data operations and success measures defined. |
| A7 — Data baseline | Week 7 | Synthetic dataset, dictionary and quality report | Reconciliation, duplicates, missing data and ownership addressed. |
| A8 — Process-mining analysis | Week 8 | Event log, mining canvas and findings | Bottlenecks, rework and priorities supported by evidence. |
| A9 — Executive dashboard | Week 9 | Dashboard v1 and management interpretation | KPIs are defined, refreshed, controlled and explained. |
| A10 — Governance pack | Week 10 | Privacy matrix, risk register and access model | POPIA, least privilege, retention, consent and audit considered. |
| A11 — Controlled proof of concept | Week 11 | Automation or AI-assisted operation with tests | Approved data only, human oversight, failure tests and limitations. |
| A12 — Capstone decision pack | Week 12 | Pilot charter, business case, RACI, roadmap and presentation | Measurable value, risks, costs, assumptions and go/no-go recommendation. |

## Scoring model

| Dimension | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Operational relevance and problem definition | 20% |
| Data quality, privacy and governance | 20% |
| Technical correctness and reproducibility | 20% |
| Measured outcome and evidence quality | 20% |
| Documentation and executive communication | 10% |
| Reflection, response to feedback and professionalism | 10% |

**Pass:** 70%  
**Distinction:** 85%

## Critical failure conditions

The assignment is placed on hold when evidence includes confidential tenant, payment or identity data; credentials or tokens; unapproved production changes; unsupported savings claims; hidden manual intervention presented as automation; missing rollback or exception handling; or deliberate avoidance of mentor review.
